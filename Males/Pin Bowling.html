Pin Bowling

Ten-pin bowling is a type of bowling in which a bowler rolls a bowling ball down a wood or synthetic lane toward ten pins positioned evenly in four rows in an equilateral triangle. The objective is to knock down all ten pins on the first roll of the ball (a strike), or failing that, on the second roll (a spare).An approximately 15 feet (5 m) long approach area used by the bowler to impart speed and apply rotation to the ball ends in a foul line.

How To Play Pin Bowling
  1. Understand the bowling lane. Before you begin to bowl, you have to understand the function of the bowling lane.If a ball veers off the lane, it goes into the gutters and is out of play.
  2. Understand the bowling pins. Ten pins are arranged at the end of the bowling lane at the beginning of every frame. They are arranged in a triangle formation, with the point of the triangle facing the bowler. There is one pin in the first row, which is the head pin, two pins in the second row, three in the third, and four in the fourth.
  3. Understand how a game of bowling works. One bowling game consists of 10 frames. Each frame is equal to one turn for the bowler. The bowler's objective is to knock down as many pins as possible in a frame, ideally all of them.
  4. Learn the scoring. If a bowler has an open frame, then they simply get credit for the number of pins they knocked down. If a bowler knocked down 6 pins after two turns, they simply get two turns.
Time Duration Of Game :

It depends on how many player are playing but a game of bowling takes approximately 10 minutes per person.

Bowling Terms :

Before you can call yourself a true bowler, you should be aware of a few different bowling terms. Knowing these terms will also make it much easier for you to understand the rules. Here they are:

  • A strike is when you knock down all the pins with the ball on your first try.
  • A spare is when you knock down all the pins on your second try.
  • If any pins remain after the bowler's turn, it's called an "open frame".
  • A turkey is three strikes in a row.
  • A split is when the first ball of a frame knocks down the headpin (the pin closest to you) but leaves two or more pins that are non-adjacent. It's tough to hit a spare in this situation, especially if you have a 7-10 split, which is the hardest split to hit.

Foosball

Table football, also known as foosball, table soccer, totó or pebolim in Brazil, futbolito in Mexico, Taca Taca in Chile and Metegol in Argentina is a table-top game that is loosely based on association football. The aim of the game is to move the ball into the opponent's goal by manipulating rods which have figures attached. Human foosball was first heard of in the 1970s but did not start becoming popular until the 2000s . Multiple people claim to have created the human foosball game, so it is uncertain if anyone came up with the idea. However, in different places around the world, human foosball tournaments are happening.

How To Play Female Foosball

  1. Players flip a coin to determine who goes first.
  2. Balls are served through the serving hole.
  3. Game play continues until someone scores, the ball is hit off the table, a team calls for a time out, or the ball is declared “dead”.
  4. To score a goal, the only requirement is that the ball must enter the goal. It still counts as a point even if the ball bounces out of the goal after scoring.
  5. Shoot the ball within 10 seconds of possession on the midfield row and within 15 seconds on the others.
  6. First team to 10 points wins the match. The losing team serves up the ball for the next round.

The official rules for the game are as follows:
  1. Players must hold onto the horizontal rods with both hands at all times.
  2. Players are not allowed to hang from the rods.
  3. It is a foul if any player, intentionally or not, is hit with the ball above the shoulders.
  4. No hands in the playing field unless a ball has been declared “dead”.
  5. No touching your opponent’s rods.
  6. No spinning of your rods to advance the ball.
  7. No distracting your opponent—keep it sportsmanlike!
  8. No moving the foosball table, banging on the sides, or slamming rods back and forth against the table walls (called “jarring”).
Variations of Foosball
  • One-on One – The most common way to play table soccer is one on one (AKA singles). Each player controls all four bars for their team and must switch between defense and offense as the ball moves.
  • Two-on-Two – Another common variation is two on two (AKA doubles). In this table soccer variation, each player keeps his or her hands on the same bars the whole time. This allows each player to focus on one duty: defense or offense. Switching positions is only allowed in-between ball serves or when an official time out has been called.
  • Goalie War– A less common variation is “goalie war,” a one-on-one game where players use only their defenders.
